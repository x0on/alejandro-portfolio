# Alejandro Renteria Press Kit — Design Brainstorm

Since this project requires **9 switchable artistic themes**, the brainstorm focuses on the **default/base design system** and the **structural layout** that will persist across all themes. Each theme will override colors, typography, and decorative elements via CSS variables.

## Idea 1: "The Director's Cut" — Editorial Film Strip Layout

**Design Movement:** Editorial / Cinematic Modernism
**Core Principles:**
1. Asymmetric editorial layouts inspired by film festival programs and director's lookbooks
2. Strong typographic hierarchy using dramatic scale contrasts
3. Cinematic framing — content presented as "scenes" with deliberate pacing
4. Negative space as a storytelling device

**Color Philosophy:** Deep charcoal base (#1a1a1a) with warm amber accents (#d4a574) — evoking the warmth of analog film projection against a dark screening room. Each theme overrides this palette entirely.

**Layout Paradigm:** A vertical scroll with a persistent left sidebar acting as a "scene list" (like chapters in a film). The main content area uses a staggered, editorial grid — not centered. Hero sections bleed to edges. Content blocks alternate between full-width cinematic moments and contained editorial columns.

**Signature Elements:**
1. A horizontal "film strip" progress indicator at the very top of the page
2. Section transitions that use horizontal wipe animations (like film cuts)
3. Pull-quotes styled as screenplay dialogue formatting

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions feel like navigating a director's personal archive — deliberate, curated, unhurried. Hover states reveal additional context like film stills appearing in a lightbox.

**Animation:** Sections fade in with a slight parallax drift. Theme transitions use a "shutter" effect — a brief black flash between themes. Sidebar items slide in sequentially on load.

**Typography System:** Display: "Playfair Display" (serif, dramatic). Body: "Source Sans 3" (clean, readable). Monospace accents for metadata (runtime, specs).

## Idea 2: "The Architect's Blueprint" — Structural Grid System

**Design Movement:** Deconstructivist Architecture meets Swiss International Style
**Core Principles:**
1. Exposed structural elements — visible grid lines, alignment markers, section labels in margins
2. Information density balanced with breathing room
3. Modular components that reconfigure per theme
4. The website itself as a "system" — reflecting Alejandro's identity as a systems thinker

**Color Philosophy:** Off-white (#f5f2eb) with graphite (#2d2d2d) and a single accent color per theme. The base feels like architectural vellum paper — technical but warm.

**Layout Paradigm:** A visible 12-column grid with content that intentionally breaks the grid at key moments. The left sidebar shows section navigation with thin connecting lines (like a blueprint legend). Content sections use varied column spans — some 4-col, some 8-col, some full-bleed — creating visual rhythm without repetition.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin hairline grid overlay that subtly appears on hover (like architectural blueprints)
2. Section numbers displayed large in the margin (01, 02, 03...)
3. "Specification blocks" for technical details styled like architectural callouts

**Interaction Philosophy:** Precise and intentional. Hover states highlight the grid structure. Clicking feels like selecting elements in a design tool. Everything has a coordinate.

**Animation:** Elements assemble on scroll — sliding in from their grid positions. Theme changes rotate through with a "blueprint redraw" effect. Minimal but precise motion.

**Typography System:** Display: "Space Grotesk" (geometric, modern). Body: "IBM Plex Sans" (technical clarity). Labels: "IBM Plex Mono" (specifications and metadata).

## Idea 3: "The Seeker's Journal" — Layered Narrative Interface

**Design Movement:** Literary Brutalism meets Wabi-Sabi Digital
**Core Principles:**
1. Content as layers — information reveals itself progressively, like turning pages
2. Textural richness — subtle grain, paper-like surfaces, ink-like typography
3. Duality of the polished and the raw — reflecting the "Manager vs. Witness" theme from his manuscript
4. Intentional imperfection as a design choice

**Color Philosophy:** Warm ivory (#faf7f2) with deep ink black (#0d0d0d) and muted earth tones. The palette evokes a well-worn journal — lived in, personal, authentic. Accent colors shift dramatically per theme.

**Layout Paradigm:** A single-column narrative flow with the sidebar as a "table of contents" pinned to the left edge. Content sections are "entries" — some with full-bleed imagery, others with tight typographic compositions. The hero is a dramatic full-viewport statement. Cards and press items use overlapping, slightly rotated layouts (like photos pinned to a board).

**Signature Elements:**
1. A subtle paper texture overlay across all backgrounds
2. "Marginalia" — small annotations that appear in the left margin on hover
3. Handwritten-style accent marks and underlines on key phrases

**Interaction Philosophy:** Intimate and exploratory. The site feels like browsing someone's personal creative archive. Modals open like unfolding letters. Expandable sections feel like opening a notebook.

**Animation:** Gentle fade-and-rise on scroll. Theme transitions use a "page turn" metaphor — a brief fold effect. Elements have a slight organic wobble on hover (imperfect, human).

**Typography System:** Display: "Cormorant Garamond" (elegant, literary). Body: "Lora" (warm serif, readable). Accents: "Caveat" (handwritten feel for annotations).

## Selected Approach: Idea 1 — "The Director's Cut"

This approach best reflects Alejandro's identity as a filmmaker and systems thinker. The editorial cinematic layout creates a sophisticated, professional press kit feel while the persistent sidebar navigation and staggered content blocks provide excellent usability. The dark base palette with warm accents perfectly suits his work in film, and the "scene-based" navigation metaphor aligns with his storytelling practice.

The 9 themes will each completely transform the visual identity while maintaining this structural foundation:
- **Glassmorphism:** Frosted glass panels, blur effects, translucent layers
- **Neubrutalism:** Bold borders, raw colors, exposed structure
- **Minimalist:** Maximum whitespace, monochrome, essential elements only
- **Swiss Design:** Grid-perfect, Helvetica-inspired, red accents
- **Bauhaus:** Primary colors, geometric shapes, functional beauty
- **Memphis:** Playful patterns, bold colors, squiggly lines
- **Retro Futurism:** Neon gradients, scan lines, chrome effects
- **Cyberpunk:** Glitch effects, neon on dark, terminal aesthetics
- **Art Deco:** Gold accents, geometric patterns, luxury typography
